# Bridge Design Worksheet

## Instructions
Complete each step before moving to the next.
Do not write the bridge statement until Steps 1–4 are complete.
The bridge statement must emerge from the gap — it cannot be written before the gap is defined.

## Step 2 — Identify and Validate the Gap

**Draft gap statement:**
> "After completing [lead magnet title], the prospect has [deliverable].
> This reveals that [larger problem].
> This larger problem cannot be solved with [lead magnet] because [honest reason]."

Fill in:
Lead magnet title: _______________
Deliverable: _______________
Larger problem: _______________
Honest reason: _______________

**Gap reality check:**
Is this gap real — created by solving the bounded problem — or manufactured by withholding?
[ ] Real — the gap emerges naturally from the delivery
[ ] Manufactured — content was deliberately withheld to create the gap

If manufactured: return to Step 1 and redesign the delivery zone.

**Gap desirability check:**
Does the prospect want to solve the larger problem?
[ ] Yes — strongly
[ ] Yes — somewhat
[ ] Unclear — need to check Desire Map

If unclear: cross-reference with Desire Map (skill 01.04) before proceeding.

## Step 4 — Select Bridge Model

**Review the three models and select the best fit:**

[ ] Model A — Bounded Solution Bridge
    Best if: Paid offer is deeper/broader version of same work
    Indicator: The lead magnet solves a specific component; paid offer addresses the system

[ ] Model B — Foundation Bridge
    Best if: Paid offer is the advanced or done-for-you version
    Indicator: Lead magnet produces a starting asset; paid offer deploys or scales it

[ ] Model C — Taste Bridge
    Best if: Paid offer is a complete program or coaching container
    Indicator: Lead magnet creates a genuine partial transformation; paid offer completes it

## Step 6 — Write the Bridge Statement

**Template:**
> "You now have [specific deliverable]. This gives you [specific benefit]. What it also reveals — and what most [identity] discover at this point — is that [the larger problem]. [Honest reason why the lead magnet doesn't address it]. If you want to [address that larger problem], [paid offer name] is built specifically for that. [One-line description of what it delivers]. You can [learn more / get started] at [link or CTA]."

**Bridge statement checks:**
[ ] 3–5 sentences — no more
[ ] Tone is matter-of-fact, not promotional
[ ] No urgency or scarcity language
[ ] Gap is presented as honest information, not a sales setup
[ ] Paid offer is named but not pitched
[ ] Next step is clear and specific

---

## Anti-Bridge Final Checks

[ ] Delivery is complete — nothing withheld to force purchase
[ ] Bridge statement is informational, not promotional
[ ] Gap is revealed through content logic, not announced from outside
[ ] Paid offer is introduced as next step, not as a product pitch
[ ] Bridge model matches the paid offer relationship
[ ] Desire gradient plan has 3+ reference moments before the bridge statement
