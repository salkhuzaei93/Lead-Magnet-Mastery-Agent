## Decision Tree

```
START: What type of promise does the lead magnet make?
│
├── "You will KNOW something" (information promise)
│   ├── Complex, needs explaining → F1 (Report) or F5 (Video)
│   └── Simple, can be listed → F2 (Checklist) or F3 (Template)
│
├── "You will BE ABLE TO DO something" (skill promise)
│   ├── Process-driven → F2 (Checklist) or F12 (Diagnostic)
│   └── Asset-dependent → F3 (Template) or F11 (Script)
│
├── "You will HAVE something" (output promise)
│   ├── Written asset → F3 (Template) or F11 (Script)
│   └── Calculated result → F10 (Calculator)
│
├── "You will UNDERSTAND your situation" (diagnostic promise)
│   └── Always → F7 (Quiz) or F12 (Diagnostic)
│
└── "You will EXPERIENCE something" (transformation promise)
    ├── Short window → F9 (Challenge)
    └── Immersive → F6 (Video Series) or F8 (Webinar)
```

## Format Negative Signal Override Copy

When a format carries negative signals, use these copy patterns to counter them:

**"This will take too long" (F1, F5, F6, F8, F9)**
> "You can complete this in [specific time]."
> "Each section is designed for a [X]-minute reading block."
> "Watch at 1.5x speed — the core insight hits in the first 8 minutes."

**"This is for everyone, not me" (F1, F2)**
> "This is specifically for [narrow recipient description]."
> "If you're not [identity], this probably isn't for you."
> Add a quiz question on the opt-in page to create personalization signal.

**"This was cheap to produce" (F2 without design)**
> Invest in premium design. A checklist that looks like a polished printed document changes the perception.
> Mention the research or experience behind it: "20 years of testing compressed into one page."

**"I have to do something every day" (F4, F9)**
> "Each day is 10 minutes or less."
> "Miss a day? No problem — each session stands alone."
> Community/accountability signal: "You're not doing this alone."
