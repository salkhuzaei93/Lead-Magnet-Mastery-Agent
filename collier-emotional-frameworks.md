# Collier Emotional Frameworks — Extended Reference

## The Collier Principle in Full

Robert Collier wrote in 1937: *"The first thing to do is to find out what the average reader of your letter already thinks, what emotions are already stirred within him regarding this subject."*

The operative word is **already**. The emotion exists before your copy arrives. Your copy does not create emotion — it identifies, validates, and redirects emotion that is already in motion. This is the difference between manipulation and resonance.

Manipulation tries to inject emotion. Resonance proves to the reader that you already understand the emotion they carry. Resonance wins.

## Emotional Profiles by Common Niche

### Online Business / Make Money Online

**Most common dominant emotion:** Frustrated hope (long-term frustration mixed with persistent belief it's possible)

After years of consuming content and trying methods, this market has a specific emotional cocktail:
- They believe it's possible (they've seen proof)
- They doubt it's possible *for them specifically*
- They're suspicious of anyone who claims to have the answer
- They secretly fear the problem is them, not the methods

**Dominant:** Self-doubt disguised as skepticism  
**Suppressed:** Shame (they haven't told anyone how much they've spent on courses)  
**Aspirational:** Quiet confidence and financial freedom  
**Copy note:** Never promise "easy" — it activates their cynicism. Promise "clear" and "specific."

---

### Health / Weight Loss

**Most common dominant emotion:** Shame with a protective layer of resignation

This is one of the most emotionally charged markets. The attempt history is typically long and painful. Every failed diet adds to a core belief: "My body doesn't work the way normal people's do."

**Dominant:** Shame + learned helplessness  
**Suppressed:** Grief (for the body they used to have, or the life they feel they're missing)  
**Aspirational:** Feeling normal, effortless, free — not "ripped," just not fighting their body every day  
**Copy note:** "This isn't your fault" is the single most powerful phrase structure in this niche. Never use before/after images without being extremely careful about the emotional signal they send.

---

### Relationships / Dating

**Most common dominant emotion:** Loneliness + confusion, often mixed with self-blame

The problem feels uniquely personal — they can't blame a system or a method. It feels like a verdict on who they are.

**Dominant:** Loneliness + self-blame  
**Suppressed:** Fear of being permanently unlovable  
**Aspirational:** Belonging, being chosen, feeling secure  
**Copy note:** Never frame the prospect as broken. Frame the situation as solvable and the missing piece as external (skill, knowledge, approach) — never internal (personality, worthiness).

---

### B2B / Business Growth (Coaches, Consultants, Agency Owners)

**Most common dominant emotion:** Overwhelm + quiet desperation

These are sophisticated, high-achieving people who don't want to admit they're struggling. The dominant emotion is often hidden beneath a performance of competence.

**Dominant:** Overwhelm + performance pressure  
**Suppressed:** Fear of being exposed as not as successful as they appear  
**Aspirational:** Control, leverage, systems that work without them  
**Copy note:** Speak peer-to-peer, never teacher-to-student. They will reject anything that implies they need to be taught basics. Frame the insight as something even experienced operators miss.

---

### Personal Finance / Investing

**Most common dominant emotion:** Anxiety + regret

The combination of watching time pass (they should have started sooner) and market volatility (they've lost money or missed gains) creates a persistent background anxiety.

**Dominant:** Anxiety about the future + regret about the past  
**Suppressed:** Embarrassment about not knowing things they feel they should know  
**Aspirational:** Security, certainty, feeling in control of their financial future  
**Copy note:** Never make them feel stupid. Frame missing knowledge as something the financial system deliberately obscured, not something they failed to learn.

---

### Parenting

**Most common dominant emotion:** Guilt + inadequacy

Parents in this space are typically consuming parenting content *because* they feel they're failing. That means the act of seeking help is already tinged with shame.

**Dominant:** Guilt + love (the two are inseparably linked here)  
**Suppressed:** Anger (at the child, at the situation, at themselves — all feel unacceptable to admit)  
**Aspirational:** Connection, ease, confidence as a parent  
**Copy note:** Open with radical validation. "You're here because you care" before anything else. Never imply the parent is the problem — always frame the situation, behavior, or approach as the variable.

## The Emotional Vocabulary Bank

High-resonance words by dominant emotion:

**Frustration vocabulary:** finally, real, actual, tired, enough, done, fix, stop, break free, clear, straight  
**Fear/Anxiety vocabulary:** safe, step-by-step, simple, proven, clear, certain, guide, protect, calm, steady  
**Shame vocabulary:** normal, common, not your fault, most people, system, hidden, nobody told you, quietly  
**Skepticism vocabulary:** proof, exactly, specific, data, case study, real, how it actually works, no fluff, honest  
**Desire/Excitement vocabulary:** imagine, possible, picture, transform, breakthrough, discover, unlock, finally  
**Envy vocabulary:** just like you, others are already, your turn, also available, same results, no different

## Emotional Transition Patterns

These are sentence structures that move the reader from their current emotional state toward the aspirational one:

**Frustration → Hope:**
> "If you've spent [time] doing [thing] and still not seeing [result] — what you're about to read will finally explain why, and more importantly, what actually works."

**Shame → Permission:**
> "Most [identity] never figure this out — not because they're not smart or dedicated enough, but because this specific piece of the puzzle is almost never taught."

**Skepticism → Curiosity:**
> "I'm not going to ask you to believe anything. I'm going to show you exactly [specific thing], and you can decide what it means."

**Fear → Safety:**
> "You don't have to figure this out alone, and you don't have to do everything at once. Here's exactly where to start."

**Envy → Inclusion:**
> "[Number] people just like you — [specific identity descriptor] — are already using this. Here's what they know that most [identity] don't."
